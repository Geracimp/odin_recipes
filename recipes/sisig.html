Sisig

Description

Sisig is a flavorful pork dish originating from the Phillipines. It is traditionally made from the "less than desirable" cuts of the pig, such as the head and ears and is typically served minced with chopped onions and chicken liver on a sizzling cast iron platter.

In America, it is common to substitute the pig's head and ears for pork belly. The meat is marinated in a combination of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic and calamansi (a filipino lime) overnight, and then it is slow roasted or smoked before being minced and seared on the grill. The result is a savory, sweet and tangy flavor that is accentuated by the sourness of calamansi and bite of garlic and onion.

Ingredients

  • 2 Lbs Pork Belly
  • 2 large red onions
  • 6 cloves of garlic
  • 4 Calamansi
  • 1/2 cup Soy Sauce
  • 1/3 cup Sukang Iloca (fermented cane sugar vinegar)
  • Season with salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Marinate pork belly in soy sauce, Sukang Iloca, Calamnsi, garlic, salt, and pepper (12-24 hrs)
  2. Preheat oven to 250 degrees Fahrenheit
  3. Slow cook in dutch oven with lid on for 8 hours
  4. Mince pork and sear on grill/skillet with chopped onions
  5. Serve sizzling hot with fresh squeeze of Calamansi juice
